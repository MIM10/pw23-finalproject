Gili Ketapang

Gili Ketapang is a small island located in the Madura Strait, precisely 8 km off the north coast of Probolinggo, East Java. The island has an area of about 68 hectares and is famous for its amazing underwater beauty. Gili Ketapang has a charming white sandy beach and is often used as a snorkeling spot. The island is also known for its beautiful natural panorama, with a coast decorated with white sand as far as the eye can see.

According to hereditary stories from the local community, Gili Ketapang used to be an area that became one with the mainland of Probolinggo, namely with Ketapang village and was only separated by a river. However, due to an earthquake caused by the eruption of Mount Semeru, the land was separated by 5 miles and headed for the middle of the ocean. The separated land was then named Gili Ketapang. This name uses the Madurese language, where Gili means flowing and Ketapang is the name of the village when it was still united. So if interpreted, Gili Ketapang is Ketapang that flows. Until now, many local residents believe that this exotic island is still moving away slowly into the middle of the ocean.

To get to Gili Ketapang Island, it can be reached easily because its location is in the middle of East Java Province, precisely in Ketapang Village, Sumberasih District, Probolinggo Regency, East Java. Tourists departing from Surabaya, Sidoarjo, or Malang need about 2 hours drive by motorized vehicle to get to the meeting point location at Tanjung Copper West Port Probolinggo. After that, they can continue the sea journey by boat to Gili Ketapang Island. Gili Ketapang offers exciting tour packages at affordable prices, so it won't burn a hole in your pocket. This island also has a mystery from the myths that developed in East Java, which adds to its attractiveness as a tourist destination. So, Gili Ketapang is a small island located in the Madura Strait, East Java. The island is famous for its underwater beauty, white sandy beaches, and beautiful natural panoramas. The island also has an interesting history and mystery that makes it even more interesting to visit.
