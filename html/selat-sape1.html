Selat Sape

Sape Strait is a strait located in Indonesia, separating Sumbawa Island to the west and Komodo Island to the east. It is also the boundary between the provinces of West Nusa Tenggara and East Nusa Tenggara. The Sape Strait is famous for its rough waters, spectacular coral reefs and amazing marine life. Therefore, the strait is increasingly popular among divers and diving enthusiasts.

The Sape Strait also has an important value in the tourism sector. Labuan Bajo Village, located in Komodo Sub-district, West Manggarai Regency, East Nusa Tenggara Province, is directly adjacent to the Sape Strait. Labuan Bajo is one of the super-priority tourist destinations being developed by the Indonesian Government. It is famous for its Komodo National Park, which has been designated as a world heritage site by UNESCO.

The Sape Strait is also known for its waters with strong currents, beautiful corals and spectacular marine life. Many divers and diving enthusiasts are keen to explore the underwater beauty around the strait. For those who love diving, the Sape Strait offers an unforgettable experience. In addition, the Sape Strait is also an important transportation route. Phinisi boats are often used as a mode of transportation to reach Sumba from Sumbawa, navigating the fierce Sape Strait. With its stunning natural beauty, waters rich in marine life, and its historical value as part of Komodo National Park, the Sape Strait offers an exceptional travel experience for its visitors.
